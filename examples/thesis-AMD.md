**AMD — Investment Thesis**

---

**SETUP**

AMD has been re-rated down from peak AI euphoria as the market concluded NVIDIA's CUDA moat is unassailable, pricing AMD as a distant second in AI GPUs with no path to meaningful share. The misunderstanding is treating AMD as a binary AI GPU trade when the real earnings engine — EPYC server CPUs systematically taking share from a structurally impaired Intel — is underappreciated, high-visibility, and carries none of the ROCm execution risk.

---

**BULL CASE** → Price target $220, 18–24 months

- **MI350/MI400 traction with hyperscalers**: Microsoft and Meta publicly commit to multi-generational MI-series deployments as a supply diversification mandate against NVIDIA concentration risk; ROCm closes enough of the CUDA gap for inference workloads; data center GPU revenue inflects to $10B+ run rate in FY2027
- **EPYC reaches 35% server CPU share**: Intel's 18A process delays push another generation of enterprise refreshes to AMD; Genoa/Turin TCO advantage widens; each 5 points of share gain adds ~$2.5B in high-margin revenue
- **PC/embedded recovery + console**: Gaming GPU and client segments return to growth; Ryzen AI gains traction in AI PC cycle; blended revenue hits $35B in FY2027 with 30%+ operating margins

---

**BASE CASE** → Price target $155, 18–24 months | Expected return ~50% vs S&P ~20%

- MI-series stays at 15–20% of incremental AI GPU spend — meaningful but not transformative; ROCm good enough for inference, not training
- EPYC reaches 30% server CPU share by end of 2026; Intel stabilizes but doesn't recover lost ground
- Revenue grows ~18% annually; operating margins expand to 27–29%; EPS of ~$6.50 in FY2027 at 24x = $155

---

**BEAR CASE** → Max drawdown -45% to ~$58 | Partially recoverable

- **ROCm fails to scale**: CUDA's software moat proves durable; hyperscalers don't diversify; AI GPU revenue stays subscale and consensus estimates are cut 25–30% — the multiple collapses because the growth story collapses with it
- **Intel executes**: 18A delivers a competitive Xeon; EPYC share gains stall at ~25%; AMD's highest-margin growth driver slows and the CPU bull case gets repriced
- **Macro/CapEx pullback**: Hyperscaler AI infrastructure spend pauses in a recession; AMD has less backlog protection than NVIDIA and more consumer cyclicality; gaming and PC roll over simultaneously

Recovery: yes if CPU thesis intact — but takes 2–3 years to re-earn the multiple.
