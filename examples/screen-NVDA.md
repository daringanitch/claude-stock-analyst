## NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) — Company Screen

---

**1. Business Model**

NVIDIA designs and sells GPUs, networking equipment, and software platforms primarily to data centers, cloud providers, and AI researchers; customers pay for hardware upfront and increasingly for recurring software/platform access (CUDA ecosystem, AI Enterprise, DGX Cloud). Revenue is driven by hyperscaler CapEx cycles: when Microsoft, Meta, Google, and Amazon build out AI infrastructure, NVIDIA captures a disproportionate share of that spend.

**2. Revenue Breakdown** *(FY2025 estimated)*

| Segment | Revenue | % of Total |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Data Center (AI/HPC) | ~$87B | ~88% |
| Gaming | ~$9B | ~9% |
| Professional Visualization | ~$1.5B | ~2% |
| Automotive | ~$1B | ~1% |

*Data Center has grown from 50% to 88% of revenue in 2 years — concentration risk and growth engine simultaneously.*

**3. Unit Economics**

*Margins at these levels on a hardware business are essentially unheard of — a function of CUDA lock-in pricing power.*

**4. Competitive Moat**

| Source | Rating (1–5) | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Pricing power | **5** | Customers pay premium and wait in queue — no credible substitute |
| Switching costs | **5** | CUDA ecosystem: millions of developers, 20 years of libraries, retraining cost is enormous |
| Network effects | **4** | More CUDA developers → more frameworks → more customers locked in |
| Scale | **5** | TSMC allocation priority, CoWoS packaging relationships, supply chain leverage |

**5. Key Risks**

1. **Customer concentration + vertical integration**: Microsoft, Google, Meta, and Amazon are simultaneously NVIDIA's largest customers and its most funded potential competitors (custom silicon: TPUs, Trainium, Maia, MTIA)
2. **Export controls**: US restrictions on H100/A100 sales to China have already cost billions; further tightening could remove 20%+ of addressable market
3. **Cyclicality**: Data center CapEx is inherently lumpy; if hyperscalers pause AI buildout, NVIDIA has no recurring revenue buffer — the last cycle (2022–2023) saw gaming/crypto collapse take the stock down 65%

**6. Bull Sentence**

If AI inference scales to consumer applications at the rate training scaled to hyperscalers, NVIDIA's installed base and CUDA moat make it the toll road on the most consequential infrastructure buildout in a generation — and we're still in the first inning.

**7. Bear Sentence**

NVIDIA is a cyclical hardware company trading at 35x forward earnings on the assumption that its largest customers don't eventually route around it — and every one of those customers has a dedicated chip team and a financial incentive to reduce the bill.

---

**Action signal: Watch** — extraordinary business, but entry point matters enormously; wait for a CapEx-pause-driven pullback of 25–30% before sizing up.
