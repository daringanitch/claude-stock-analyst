### 3. CONCENTRATION RISK

Top 10 holdings = ~35% of the portfolio. Top 5 = ~26%.

**Flag:** VOO markets itself as diversification across 500 companies, but it is functionally a concentrated tech fund with other assets attached. A 20% drawdown in AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, AMZN, and META — five stocks — moves the entire fund by ~5 percentage points.

The S&P 500 is market-cap weighted, which means past winners grow into larger weights automatically. NVDA went from ~1% to ~6% of the index in 24 months. This is a feature (you ride winners) and a risk (you're most exposed to whatever ran up most recently).

### 6. VERDICT

**VOO is the right core holding for most long-term investors.** It is the cheapest, most liquid, most tax-efficient way to own US large-cap equities. The "diversification" is real but overstated — you are making a concentrated bet on US mega-cap tech at current market-cap weights, and that bet has been correct for 15 years.

**Own it if:** You want broad US equity exposure at minimal cost and are comfortable with tech concentration.

**Supplement it with** small-cap (VB, IWM) or international (VXUS, EFA) exposure if you want true diversification beyond US large-cap.

**Don't expect it to:** Protect you in a tech-led selloff. In a scenario where NVDA, AAPL, MSFT, and META all correct 30%, VOO corrects ~10% even if the other 490 stocks are flat.
