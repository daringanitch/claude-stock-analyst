## 6. BLIND SPOTS

**Blind spot 1 — Taiwan is a single-event binary risk across two positions**
TSM and NVDA together represent ~18% of the portfolio and share an unhedgeable tail risk: a Taiwan Strait escalation. This isn't a slow-moving risk — it's an overnight gap-down event. Neither diversification nor stop-losses help. You either accept the exposure knowingly or reduce one of them.

**Blind spot 2 — You own zero healthcare**
No healthcare is unusual in a diversified portfolio. Pharma, medtech, and managed care are among the lowest-correlation sectors to tech and industrials. A recession, a rotation out of AI, or a rate spike all hurt this portfolio — healthcare would buffer at least one of those scenarios.

**Blind spot 3 — YPF is not an oil stock, it's an Argentina bet**
YPF trades on Argentine political news, peso devaluation, and IMF relationship — not on oil prices. It has been expropriated once (2012) and operates under capital controls. If it's in the portfolio as an oil/energy play, it's the wrong instrument. If it's a deliberate high-conviction EM/Argentina recovery bet, that's fine — but size it as a speculative position, not a core holding.

**Blind spot 4 — The AI CapEx thesis is load-bearing for 27%+ of the portfolio**
NVDA + TSM + AMZN are all dependent on hyperscaler AI infrastructure spend continuing. If Meta, Microsoft, or Google signal a CapEx pause, this portfolio loses 8–15% in a day from those three names alone.
